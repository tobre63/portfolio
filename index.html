01

Problem

The psychological horror market is dominated by titles with high graphical demands, often excluding players with lower-end hardware. My challenge for this final school project (PAP) was to bridge this gap by developing an immersive, accessible 2D experience. The goal was to prove that tension and psychological dread could be achieved through intelligent art direction and sound design rather than high-end processing power.

02

Process

The project involved full-stack development, using Unity and C# for the game engine and Aseprite for original pixel art, with key technical milestones including immersive mechanics such as a unique system where the game detects real-world audio via the user's microphone to alert the antagonist, system architecture through the design of a dynamic task manager using LIFO (Last-In, First-Out) stack logic and NPC AI with custom pathfinding, and a web ecosystem built from scratch using HTML5, CSS3, PHP, and MySQL to handle user authentication and ensure secure distribution and safe downloads.

03

Result

The final product is a polished, 20~minute horror experience featuring 100% original code, art, and design. The project successfully met all technical objectives, demonstrating that a fluid and terrifying experience is viable on systems with minimal requirements. The project received a final grade of 18/20 from the jury.

01

Brief

The club's existing visual identity lacked consistency and failed to reflect its true character and presence. I took the initiative to propose a complete visual overhaul, presenting multiple concepts across Instagram posts, stories, banners, and club graphics. The objective was clear: create a bold, modern identity while respecting the club's heritage — giving athletes and supporters something they can wear and represent with pride.

02

Process

The process began with research into well-structured and visually strong clubs, identifying what set them apart in terms of branding and communication. In parallel, I worked to understand the club's expectations, presenting multiple design directions and iterations to refine the visual approach. A cohesive system was built with consistency and adaptability in mind, supported by a colour palette designed to stand out both on screen and in real-world applications. Every decision aimed to elevate the club to a more professional and organized visual standard.
